# On Forward — Prospect List Build Criteria
## Target: 200–300 contacts, ready to load when warm-up completes

---

## Primary vertical: Professional Services

**Why this first:** Our strongest case study (reporting agent, 40% more clients, no headcount) maps directly to this segment. The pain is universal and the ROI is obvious to any managing partner.

### Who to find

**Company filters:**
- Industry: Management consulting, accounting/advisory, financial advisory, boutique law (commercial/corporate), engineering consulting, HR consulting
- Employee count: 30–300
- Location: UK, Netherlands, Germany, Ireland, Denmark, Sweden, Norway (English-speaking ops)
- Revenue signal: established firms, not one-person shops

**Person filters (find one of these per company — in priority order):**
1. COO / Chief Operating Officer
2. Managing Partner / Managing Director
3. Head of Operations / Operations Director
4. CFO (at accounting firms — they feel the reporting pain)
5. Practice Manager / Office Manager (smaller firms)

**Avoid:**
- Big 4 / Top 10 accounting firms (too slow, too much procurement)
- Single-person consultancies (no budget, no problem worth solving)
- Firms where the founder IS the operations (no one to champion internally)

---

## Secondary vertical: Logistics & Freight (load after first batch)

**Why second:** Our logistics case study (invoice-to-PO matching, 3 days/week → automated) is very specific and lands well with ops-heavy freight companies.

**Company filters:**
- Industry: Freight forwarding, 3PL/third-party logistics, customs brokerage, supply chain consulting
- Employee count: 50–500
- Location: Netherlands, Germany, Belgium, Denmark, UK (strong logistics hubs)

**Person filters:**
1. VP Operations / Head of Operations
2. COO
3. Finance Director / CFO (invoice pain lives here too)
4. Head of Finance / Controller

---

## Where to build the list (free options)

### Option A — LinkedIn (manual, highest quality)
1. Go to LinkedIn search → People
2. Filters: Title = "COO" OR "Managing Partner" OR "Head of Operations" | Industry = Management Consulting | Company size = 51–200
3. Location: United Kingdom, Netherlands, Germany (run separately)
4. Export names + companies manually into the sheet
5. Find emails via Hunter.io (free: 25 searches/mo) or Apollo.io (free tier: 50 exports/mo)

### Option B — Apollo.io (faster, still free-tier)
1. apollo.io → sign up free
2. People search → same filters as above
3. Free tier gives 50 email exports/month — enough to start
4. Export as CSV → paste into Prospects sheet

### Option C — Hunter.io Domain Search
1. hunter.io → Domain Search
2. Enter a target company's domain
3. It shows you all email addresses it's found for that domain
4. Grab the right person's email + verify

---

## List hygiene before loading

Before setting Status = Active on any row:
- [ ] Email looks real (not info@, not hello@, not contact@)
- [ ] Person is still at the company (check LinkedIn quickly)
- [ ] Company is 30–300 people (not a solo op, not a giant)
- [ ] No overlap with anyone you know personally

---

## First batch recommendation

Start with **40–50 contacts** in week 4 (when warm-up is done), not all 200 at once.
- Lets you read signal before burning the whole list
- If a subject line isn't working, you find out early and can adjust
- Set Status = Active on 50 rows → let the script run for 2 weeks → review reply rate → then load the next batch

**Target reply rate: 3–8%** on cold email is good. If you're getting less than 2%, we rewrite the copy before loading more.

---

## Tracking the pipeline

Use the Prospects sheet as your CRM:
- Replied → move to a dedicated follow-up tracker (or just filter by "Replied" in the sheet)
- Set a reminder to follow up on Replied contacts within 24 hours
- Every "Replied" is a potential discovery call — that's the next milestone
